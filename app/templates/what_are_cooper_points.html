Cooper Points are a concept developed by Dr. Kenneth H. Cooper in 1968. They are a method to measure the intensity of an activity, assigning a number of points based on distance and time expended.

The general goal is to track the number of points a person earns over a week, and the general recommendation by Dr. Cooper is to maintain a 30 points / week value to maintain healthy aerobic levels.

I built this application to help myself and others track these points with a simple and intuitive interface. Simply enter the distance and time you performed a walk, run, swim, or bike and it will calculate how many points you earned, saving it in the database.
